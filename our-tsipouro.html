Two expressions, one tradition

FROM VINE TO BOTTLE

The patience of slow distillation

Every bottle of Tsipouro Larissas begins in the same place — the Thessalian plains — and follows the same path our family has walked for three generations.

STEP 1

HARVEST

Late summer

The grapes are hand-picked from selected vineyards across the Thessalian plains. We work only with varieties that suit our craft — and only at the moment of peak ripeness. No mechanical harvest. No hurry.

STEP 2

FERMENTATION

Slow, undisturbed

The grape pomace — what remains after the wine has been pressed — ferments slowly in traditional vats. This is where the character of the spirit begins to form: aromatic, alive, ready for the still.

STEP 3

DISTILLATION

Twice, in copper

Small-batch distillation in copper stills, heated by direct flame. We distill twice — once to extract, once to refine. It is the method our grandfather used, and the method we have not changed.

STEP 4

BOTTLING

By hand, at the distillery

Every bottle is filled, sealed, and labelled at our distillery in Nikaia, Larissa. No outsourcing. No automation. The same hands that distil the spirit are the hands that bottle it.
