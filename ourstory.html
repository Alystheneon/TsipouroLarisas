Our Story

Three generations distilling the spirit of Thessaly.

The Family Behind the Bottle

A small still in the 1970s

It all started in the 1970s, when our grandfather built a small still in Larissa to serve the local tsipouradika. He distilled what the vineyards of Thessaly gave him each autumn, one copper batch at a time.

That first still is still in the building today — kept not as a museum piece, but as a reminder of where the family began.

From Larissa to All of Greece

The second generation expands

Our father took the distillery into a new era. He kept the recipe intact, but slowly opened the doors to the rest of Greece — supplying restaurants, tsipouradika and wine shops from Athens to Thessaloniki through a national distributor.

By the late nineties, a bottle from Larissa was no longer something you could only find on a Thessalian table.

A New Generation

Mihail Moutogiorgos · today

Today the distillery is run by Mihail Moutogiorgos, grandson of the founder. The facilities have been modernised, the labels redrawn, and the family is taking its first careful steps abroad — towards Europe and selected international markets.

This website is the first chapter of that new journey: an honest introduction in English to a product that has spoken Greek for fifty years.

Our Values

What we refuse to change

We are not the biggest distillery in Greece, and we do not want to be. Our tsipouro is a Protected Geographical Indication of Thessaly, distilled in copper, from grape pomace supplied by the vineyards around us.

Quality before volume. Family before brand. Place before fashion.
